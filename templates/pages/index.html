About

Shrikant Verma Trust

The Shrikant Verma Trust, established by Shrikant Verma's son Abhishek Verma in 1989, is a philanthropic foundation dedicated to supporting arts, literature, journalism, education, and community welfare. It honors the legacy of the late Shrikant Verma, a visionary poet, writer, journalist, and politician. The trust, now also known as the Shrikant Verma Charitable Trust, provides aid to the poor and weaker sections of society, carrying out initiatives in social service and cultural promotion.

About

Late Shrikant Verma

Shrikant Verma was born in 1931 in Bilaspur, Madhya Pradesh. He graduated from Nagpur University with a Master of Arts degree in Hindi.

He began his career as a journalist and worked for several leading newspapers and magazines. In 1976, he entered politics and was elected to the Indian Parliament as a member of the Indian National Congress party. He served as a Member of Parliament (Rajya Sabha) for two 6 year terms from the state of Madhya Pradesh and was the General Secretary of the Congress Party, as well as the Official Spokesman for 10 years.

He was a prolific writer and published over twenty books of poetry and prose. His poems are known for their simplicity, beauty, and social commentary.

He was awarded Tulsi Samman for Jalsagar from Madhya Pradesh Government in 1976 and Shiksha Samman from Madhya Pradesh State Kala Parishad in 1981. In 1982, he presided over the Afro-Asian Writer's Conference hosted in New Delhi. In 1987, he was posthumously awarded the Sahitya Academy Award for Magadh.
